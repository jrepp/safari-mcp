Your AI agent probably runs two browsers right now.

Your real Safari — with your cookies, your GitHub session,
your Gmail, your password manager, your 2FA tokens. The one
you actually use.

And a headless Chromium that Playwright spun up — with no
logins, no sessions, no history. A stranger.

When the agent needs to "go look something up," it drives
the stranger. Then it hits a login wall. Then it asks you
to share credentials. Then it fails anyway because 2FA.

I spent a year tired of this. So I shipped Safari MCP —
an MCP server that drives the Safari you're already logged
into. 80 tools. No Chromium. No Puppeteer. No re-login loops.

~60% less CPU than headless Chrome on Apple Silicon (measured
on my M1 Pro running identical scrape loops).

6,000+ monthly npm downloads in its first 30 days.

If you're building agents on macOS and you already have Safari
open — you're running a second browser for no reason.
